Origins of Isshinryu

Shorin Ryu means "Young Forest"
Goju Ryu means "Hard Soft"

The three styles of Shorin Ryu are:
  1. Kobayashi Ryu founded by Choshin Chibana (1887 1969)
  2. Shobayashi Ryu founded by Chotoku Kyan (1870 1945)
  3. Matsubayashi Ryu founded by Shoshin Nagamine (1907)

The three Okinawan villages where Karate was developed are:
  1. Shuri (Shuri Te Shorin Ryu)
  2. Naha (Naha Te Goju Ryu)
  3. Tomari (Tomari Te Shorin Ryu)

The Chinese influence in Isshinryu comes from Chojun Miyagi (Chinto also had an influence).

Four former U.S. Marines are credited with bringing Isshinryu to the United States: Harold Mitchum, Harold Long, Don Nagle and Steve Armstrong.
